Back To The Future: 40 Years Back And Forth In Time [B1]

Sono passati già quarant’anni dall’uscita di “Ritorno al futuro”, un film che ha resistito alla prova del tempo ed è entrato nei cuori di diverse generazioni.

Great Scott! Back to the Future is forty years old this year. The iconic movie is beloved by generations past, present and, no doubt, future, for its mix of science fiction, comedy and 1950s’ nostalgia and for exploring the consequences of altering the past and the importance of accepting one’s destiny. 

THE STORY AND CHARACTERS

Released in July 1985, Back to the Future stars young-looking twenty-three-year-old actor Michael J. Fox as teenager Marty McFly. McFly accidentally travels back to 1955 in a DeLorean, a fictional time-travelling vehicle built by eccentric scientist Emmett ‘Doc’ Brown (played by Christopher Lloyd.) While there, McFly inadvertently prevents his teenage parents from falling in love, risking his own existence. And so he has to reconcile his parents and return to the future. 

REJECTED 40+ TIMES

Robert Zemeckis directed and wrote Back to the Future with his writing partner Bob Gale. They came up with the idea in 1980, but it was rejected by over forty studios before Universal Pictures agreed to take it on. The filmmaker wanted Fox to play Marty, but Fox wasn’t available so they initially gave the role to an actor named Eric Stoltz. However, they later decided that Stoltz wasn’t right for the role and so changed the schedule to accommodate Fox. This required re-filming some of the scenes, which cost millions of dollars — money we now know was well spent! 

A PHENOMENAL SUCCESS

Back to the Future was a critical and commercial success. It made over $380 million globally in 1985 and became the highest-grossing film that year, going on to win many awards. It also inspired two sequels and a multimedia franchise that includes theme park rides, video games, an animated TV series and a theatrical musical.

BACK TO THE FUTURE DAY

There’s even an official Back to the Future Day on 21 October, as McFly travels back to that date in Back to the Future II. This year, fans will celebrate the first film all year round with special tributes and screenings, no doubt quoting famous phrases from the movie such as “Great Scott!”, “Flux capacitor” and “88 miles per hour”.
